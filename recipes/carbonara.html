Carbonara

Description

Spaghetti carbonara is a quick Italian classic. A true carbonara is made without cream and becomes creamy thanks to a splash of pasta water in the sauce. Don't skimp on the black pepper and use freshly ground from the mill

  • 400 g of spaghetti
  • 4 egg yolks
  • 200 g pancetta
  • 30 g pecorino cheese, finely grated
  • 30 g parmesan cheese, finely grated (preferably aged at least 16 months)
  • black pepper, freshly ground salt
  1. Cook the spaghetti about 3 minutes shorter than the instructions on the package say.
  2. Cut the pancetta into small pieces.
  3. Stek pancettan i en wokpanna eller en stekpanna med höga kanter utan fett tills den blivit krispig. Lyft upp och lägg åt sidan. Låt fettet vara kvar i pannan.
  4. Whisk together the egg yolks with the finely grated cheese and plenty of freshly ground black pepper in a bowl.
  5. Put the heat back on the pan with the frying fat, pour in about 3 dl of the pasta water and let it boil.
  6. Drain the spaghetti and add to the pan with the pancetta. Just like when you cook risotto in a broth, the pasta should be cooked in the frying fat. Let it boil for about 2-3 minutes and stir all the time, this causes the starch from the pasta to be secreted and it becomes a creamy sauce.
  7. When the water starts to boil and there is a tablespoon left, remove the pan from the heat. Add the egg mixture, stir until it becomes creamy, adding a little water if it becomes dry. Season with salt and pepper.
